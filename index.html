How to find the right person on LinkedIn

Don't message a company page, it goes nowhere. Find a real human on the events, community, people or marketing team. Click "Find a person on LinkedIn" on any card, or search these yourself.

What to type in LinkedIn search

Search the role, then on the results page click People, then filter by Current company (the company) and Locations (Israel or Tel Aviv).

  • Events Manager or Events Coordinator + company name
  • Community Manager or Community Lead + company name
  • Employer Brand or Employee Experience + company name
  • Social Media Manager or Content + company name
  • Talent Acquisition or Recruiter + company name (great for "is anything open?")
  • Office Manager or People & Culture + company name
  • For NGOs: Events, Fundraising, Development, or Volunteer Coordinator + org name

Quick rules

  • Aim for someone mid-level, a manager or coordinator. Junior people reply more and often refer you up.
  • Set Locations to Israel so you reach the Tel Aviv team, not the US head office.
  • Send the connection note first (under 300 characters). Only send the follow-up once they accept.
  • Add one personal line where you can, mention a specific event, post or product of theirs. It doubles reply rates.
  • Keep it to one a day. Steady beats a burst that gets you flagged as spam.
